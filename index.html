Why RUSCA?

We’re the official undergraduate supply chain organization at Rutgers Business School. Our board partners with companies, alumni, and faculty to translate coursework into experiential learning.

  • Weekly programming spanning logistics, procurement, analytics, and ops
  • Alumni insight through the Supply Chain Central podcast and blog
  • Accelerated career pipelines via externships and corporate tours
Our Mission

Cultivating Scarlet supply chain leaders since 2009

RUSCA is an undergraduate student organization dedicated to informing and engaging Rutgers students interested in Supply Chain Management through immersive learning and industry engagement.

From classroom concepts to industry impact

Founded alongside the Rutgers Business School Supply Chain Management program, RUSCA enriches academic learning with corporate spotlights, site visits, externships, and mentorship experiences. Members explore strategies in global sourcing, procurement, risk analysis, logistics, operations, sales planning, demand fulfillment, and continuous improvement—learning something new every time we meet.

As the discipline evolves, we stay on the leading edge with alumni and corporate partners who mentor students, surface internships and co-ops, and spark dialogue on the latest industry trends.

What We Do

Programs that power Scarlet success

Signature initiatives connect students with the people, insights, and experiences that make Rutgers supply chain education stand out.

Career Readiness

Resume and interview labs, alumni mentorship, and skill-building sessions designed to prepare members for internships and full-time roles.

Corporate Engagement

Spotlights, panels, and networking with Fortune 500 partners who collaborate with RUSCA for recruitment and experiential opportunities.

Leadership Development

Committee placements, project ownership, and executive board roles that cultivate confident and capable student leaders.

Signature Programming

Events that elevate every stage of the supply chain journey

Photos and highlights sourced from our event gallery showcase the energy, collaboration, and professional exposure members experience each semester.

Kickoff

General Interest Meetings (GIM)

Welcome new members, meet the executive board, and learn about committee placements, upcoming programs, and how to get involved from day one.

  • Held at the start of each semester
  • Overview of committees, leadership, and events
Industry

Corporate Spotlights

Interactive sessions with partner companies highlighting supply chain careers, current projects, and pathways to internships and full-time roles.

  • Featuring firms from logistics, retail, pharma, and tech
  • Networking and breakout discussions
Experience

Externship Program

Immersive project-based learning that pairs students with corporate partners to solve supply chain challenges and present recommendations.

  • Multi-week engagements with dedicated mentors
  • Final presentations to partner leadership teams
Immersion

Site Visits

On-location tours of distribution centers, innovation labs, and fulfillment operations that bring coursework to life and spark career ideas.

  • Travel to partner facilities across the tri-state area
  • Q&A with operations and logistics leaders
Community

Annual Banquet & Awards

A year-end celebration recognizing member achievements, announcing externship partners, and honoring graduates entering the supply chain workforce.

  • Leadership, service, and academic honors
  • Photo moments with sponsors and alumni
